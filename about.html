Our History

@glassanimals

Glass Animals are an English indie rock band formed in Oxford in 2010. Founded and led by singer, songwriter, and producer Dave Bayley, the group also features his
childhood friends Joe Seaward, Ed Irwin-Singer and Drew MacFarlane. Bayley wrote and produced all three Glass Animals albums. Bayley spent his childhood in
Massachusetts and Texas[3] before relocating to Oxford, where he met the other band members at school.[4] Their first album, Zaba (2014), spawned the single
“Gooey”, which was eventually certified platinum in the United States. Their second full album, How to Be a Human Being, received positive reviews and won in
two categories at the 2018 MPG Awards for UK Album of the Year and Self Producing Artist of the Year, as well as a spot on the Mercury Prize shortlist. The third,
Dreamland, peaked at number two on the UK Albums Chart and number seven on the US Billboard 200.

Us!

@glassanimals



Dave Algernon Bayley is the lead vocalist for Glass Animals. He was born on June 7th, 1989 and is currently aged 33. Dave was born in Massachusetts, US. After staying in Massachusetts for a while, his family moved to College Station, Texas. This is where he met one of his best friends. According to an interview with Independent UK, the two spent a lot of time discovering new music together, such as Eminem and Dr. Dre.

Drew MacFarlane is the guitarist for Glass Animals. Drew MacFarlane Born America Occupation Guitarist, keyboardist Years active 2012-present Musical career Role Guitarist, keyboardist, backing vocalist Origin Oxford, England Instruments Guitar, keyboard Associated acts Joey Bada$$ He was born on November 21st, 1988 in America. Drew met Dave and the rest of the Glass Animals members in secondary school after moving to England from America. Like Drew, the future lead singer, Dave, was also from America, and the two quickly became friends.

Joseph "Joe" Seaward is the drummer for the English psychedelic pop band, Glass Animals. Joe was born on October 22nd, 1988. He is currently 33 years old. He met the lead singer, Dave Bayley, in secondary school after Bayley moved to Oxford, UK from the USA. Along with meeting Dave in secondary school, Joe also met the other two future members of Glass Animals, Drew MacFarlane, and Edmund Irwin-Singer. Joe said he had never really intended to be a musician, but Dave managed to coax him into it, saying that they could try it out for a year and if it didn't work out, they would stop. They eventually produced their first studio album, ZABA, and then went on to make How to be a Human Being and Dreamland.

Edmund Irwin-Singer, also known as Ed, was born on November 23rd in England. He plays the bass and keys and sometimes helps with backing vocals. He is a pretty shy person compared to the rest of the group, he doesn't talk much in interviews, and he doesn't have any other job experience.
